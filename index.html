Introduction

Hello fellow travellers, This is my first article on this site dedicated to providing my fellow travellers tips from their own generation.
This guide is about you can enjoy and stay safe in the Central American country of Guatemala as travelling here without knowing the place and how to stay safe can be quite detrimental to your own safety coming to a country like this unaware. This article is catered to Australian youth ranging from the ages 18-25.

Overview

  • Guatemala is a Central American country with a tropical climate
  • Guatemala is a country with quite a high crime rate so safety is a must.
  • Be sure to watch out for the season when travelling to Guatemala as different types of weather can disallow you to enjoy certain landmarks within the country.
  • Try to stay away from mosquito hotspots as Guatemala hosts a variety of insect borne diseases.
  • If you are used to drinking tap water then do not do it here, instead drink bottled water as water in Guatemala may not be safe for consumption.
  • Some street food can be made in fairly unsanitary conditions.

What is Interesting in Guatemala?

Guatemala is a country known for its beautiful volcanic landscapes and ancient mayan sites with the country possessing a few UNESCO world heritage sites such as "Tikal national park", which is one of the sites that holds the ruins of an ancient Mayan civilization and one of the others being the historical town of Antigua. Some other things you could do is ride the "Chicken" bus which is not filled with chickens but rather filled to the brim with plenty of people in the inside, or if you want to take a dip in some water then you can visit the natural monument of Semuc Champey in its turqoise coloured waters, for the coffee enthusiasts you can take a tour at the abundance of coffee farms around the country. So from this small list of places Guatemala is quite a good country to go to if you are interested in natural features, ancient civilizations or a coffee enthusiast

How to Stay Safe and Abide by the Rules

  • Travel with an adequate amount of insect protection as insect-borne diseases are quite common in Guatemala
  • You are not allowed to take pictures of the airport,military sites,government buildings and the presidential palace.
  • Guatemala has quite a high crime rate that further increases by night time so it is advisable to make sure your flight to the country arrives during daytime, cashing out on an atm machine during the day is a must.
  • Never hitchhike or trust strangers offering a ride, only travel in radio/prepaid taxis as criminals target tourists in the airport.
  • Same sex marriage is accepted within the capital city of Guatemala although it is ill-advised to show public displays of affection outside of the capital.
  • Be wary when travelling around the volcanoes as the majority of volcanoes within Guatemala are quite active.
  • Do not eat food that is raw/undercooked as diseases in food is quite common.
  • Try not to travel to this country during the months of June-October.
  • You will need a valid yellow fever vaccination to enter the country.
  • Night time is very dangerous with highly increased rates of murder, kidnapping, carjacking and armed robbery.
  • An Australian Consulate can be found with the address of "Avenida Las Americas 7-20 Zona 13 Real America local 24" in Guatemala city.

For the Foodies

Guatemala has an unsurprisingly vast range of food which is not surprising considering how food is known within the regions of Central America and South America. Guatemala has no national dish but the food that comes as a worthy contender for such a title would be the pepian which is a meaty stew that often contains both fruits and vegetables with a lot of spices mixed into it, this is an example of fusion cuisine with this dish having Mayan and Spanish roots. Among the cheaper meals is the Pupusa which is a thick corn tortilla stuffed with a filling such as refried beans and cheese, the famous empanada which is a handy and portable snack which is basically a hand pie with vegetarian or meat fillings common throughout countries with spanish influence, Rellenitos which are deep fried plantains mixed up with refried beans sugar and cinammon this is one of their cuisine which revolves on the sweeter side. And one last fast about the cuisine, Guatemala is known as the birhtplace of chocolate as cocoa has an enormous significance within mayan culture being used as a currency in the previous times.
